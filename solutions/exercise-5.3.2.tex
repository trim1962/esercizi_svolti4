	Risolvere la seguente disequazione $\dfrac{10-2x^2-x}{2-4x}< 0$
	\begin{align*}
		2-4x>&0\\
		-4x>&-2\\
		x<&\dfrac{1}{2}\\
		x_{1,2}=&\xunodue{-2}{-1}{+10}=\dfrac{1\pm\sqrt{1+80}}{-4}\\
		=&\dfrac{1\pm\sqrt{81}}{-4}=\dfrac{1\pm 9}{-4}\\
		=&\begin{cases}
			x_1=\dfrac{-8}{-4}=2\\
			x_2=\dfrac{10}{-4}=-\dfrac{5}{2}
		\end{cases}\\
	\end{align*}
